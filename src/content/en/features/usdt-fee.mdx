## Overview

TBC Wallet's USDT Fee feature allows you to pay transaction fees using USDT (Tether) instead of native network tokens. This provides price stability and predictability, especially valuable during volatile market conditions.

## What is USDT Fee?

USDT Fee is a payment option that lets you use USDT (a stablecoin pegged to the US dollar) to cover transaction fees on supported blockchain networks. Instead of holding multiple native tokens for fees, you can use USDT across different networks.

### Benefits

- **Price Stability**: USDT maintains a stable value, avoiding fee volatility
- **Convenience**: One token for fees across multiple networks
- **Predictability**: Know exactly what fees will cost in USD terms
- **Simplicity**: No need to manage multiple native tokens

## How It Works

### Fee Payment Process

1. **Enable USDT Fees**: Activate the feature in your wallet settings
2. **Select USDT**: Choose USDT as your preferred fee payment method
3. **Execute Transaction**: Complete transactions as normal
4. **Automatic Conversion**: Fees are automatically paid from your USDT balance

### Supported Networks

USDT Fee is available on:

- **TBChain**: Native support with optimized rates
- **Ethereum**: ERC-20 USDT support
- **Tron**: TRC-20 USDT support
- **And More**: Continuously expanding network support

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is USDT Fee available on all networks?

USDT Fee is available on supported networks including TBChain, Ethereum, Tron, and others. Check the wallet interface for current network support.

### How are USDT fees converted?

USDT fees are converted at real-time market rates using secure conversion protocols. Rates are displayed before transaction confirmation.

### Are there additional fees for using USDT?

No, there are no additional fees for using USDT to pay fees. You only pay the standard network fees converted to USDT.

### Can I switch back to native token fees?

Yes, you can change your fee payment preference at any time in wallet settings.

### What happens if I don't have enough USDT?

If your USDT balance is insufficient, the wallet will prompt you to add funds or switch to native token fees.

### Is USDT Fee safe?

Yes, USDT Fee uses secure conversion protocols and includes all standard security measures. Your USDT is held securely in your wallet.
